90% of My Executive Experience Was Useless in AI Development

I spent 10 years climbing the corporate ladder, ultimately becoming the Deputy CEO of a Russian digital infrastructure program. But when I made the career pivot to become an AI developer, I was shocked to find that only about 10% of my executive experience was transferable. The remaining 90% was useless in the context of building and running a tech startup. Specifically, the $1.2 million budget I used to manage was now a distant memory, replaced by the harsh reality of bootstrapping with $0 in VC funding.

Transferring Executive Experience to Tech

The 10% of my experience that did transfer was primarily related to project management, prioritization, and problem-solving. As an executive, I was accustomed to making high-level decisions, managing teams, and navigating complex systems. These skills have been invaluable in building and deploying multi-agent systems on Oracle Cloud, where I need to prioritize tasks, manage workflows, and troubleshoot issues. For instance, when I encountered a 503 error while deploying a Groq-based routing agent, my executive experience kicked in, allowing me to quickly identify the root cause and resolve the issue.

What Was Completely Useless

On the other hand, many of the skills I developed as an executive were completely useless in the context of AI development. My experience with corporate politics, networking, and public speaking, for example, has been largely irrelevant. In fact, I've found that the more I focus on the technical aspects of building AI systems, the less time I have for these non-essential activities. This has been a welcome change, as I can now focus on what really matters: building and shipping functional AI agents. For example, my Telegram and WhatsApp agents, which utilize Claude routing, have required intense technical focus, leaving little room for corporate niceties.

The Gap Between Executive and Developer

For a long time, I tried to hide the gap between my executive experience and my newfound role as an AI developer. I was afraid that others would view me as inexperienced or unqualified. However, I've come to realize that this gap is not only natural but also necessary. By embracing my non-traditional background, I've been able to bring a unique perspective to the field of AI development. My experience with multi-agent systems, for instance, has been influenced by my understanding of complex systems and organizational dynamics. This perspective has allowed me to identify novel solutions to problems that might have gone unnoticed by more traditional developers.

Overcoming Real-World Constraints

As a solo AI builder, I face numerous constraints, including limited funding, infrastructure costs, and the need to manage multiple tasks simultaneously. To overcome these challenges, I've had to be creative and resourceful. For example, I've developed a system for deploying AI agents on Oracle Cloud that minimizes costs while maximizing efficiency. This has involved optimizing my use of Groq and Claude routing, as well as leveraging Telegram and WhatsApp to interact with users. By focusing on these practical challenges, I've been able to build a functional and scalable AI system, despite the constraints.
